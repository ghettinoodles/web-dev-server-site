The History and Future of Web Servers

So where did web servers come from?Let's take a step back from the millions of web servers that exist today and look at the very first. The very first web server was created in 1990 by Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist who worked for CERN. He is credited as the inventor of the world wide web as well as the first web server. Lee proposed the idea of a “hypertext project” called WorldWideWeb in which a web of hypertext documents could be viewed by browsers first in March of 1989, and then again in May of 1990 (Via CERN: A History of the Web).

The first web page address actually still exists and can be accessed here. The web server began to spread to the US, which first came online in December 1991. The small team at CERN reached out for help on expanding their project and by late 1993 many individuals had created user-friendly browsers. This resulted in the existence of over 500 web servers. Because website information is stored on web servers, the growth of the internet and creation of more websites created a need for more web servers. Web servers are computers comprised of hardware and software, including hard drives. This means that they do not have unlimited storage, which is one reason why multiple servers must be created. Today, web servers are stored in facilities called data centers which house hundreds or thousands of servers. It is estimated that around 5,400 of these centers currently exist in the US alone.
